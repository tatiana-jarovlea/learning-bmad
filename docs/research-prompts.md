## Research Prompt 1: Cynology Federation Landscape in Eastern Europe

### Research Objective

Map the cynology federation ecosystem in Eastern Europe — specifically Moldova and Romania — to assess partnership potential, data accessibility, and digitization status for PawTrust, a verification-first pet breeder discovery platform launching in Moldova.

### Background Context

PawTrust plans to use cynology competition records, registered kennel data, and FCI-affiliated club membership as primary trust signals for breeder verification. The platform needs to understand what data exists, how it is managed, and whether federations would partner with or endorse a digital marketplace.

### Core Research Questions

1. Which FCI-affiliated cynology federations operate in Moldova and Romania? Who are their leadership contacts?
2. Do these federations maintain digital databases of registered kennels, competition results, and titles? Are these publicly accessible or API-queryable?
3. What is the current digitization level of competition records (show titles, working titles, sport results) in Moldova and Romania specifically?
4. Have any EE cynology federations previously partnered with digital platforms or marketplaces? What were the outcomes?
5. What are the federation's concerns or objections likely to be regarding a commercial platform using their data?
6. Are there national breed registries (separate from FCI clubs) in Moldova and Romania that maintain pedigree databases?

### Supporting Questions

- How many registered kennels/breeders are active in Moldova vs. Romania?
- What is the typical annual competition calendar and how are results recorded?
- Are there informal breeder associations or groups outside FCI structure that hold influence?
- What do breeders value most about their federation membership?

### Research Methodology

- Review FCI website for affiliated member organizations in MD and RO
- Review national kennel club websites (ACR in Romania, equivalent in Moldova)
- Search for publicly available competition result databases
- Review any existing API documentation or data export capabilities
- LinkedIn/direct outreach research to identify key contacts at federations

### Output Requirements

- List of all FCI-affiliated clubs in Moldova and Romania with contact info
- Assessment of data digitization level: None / Partial / Full / API-ready
- Partnership opportunity assessment: Likelihood and mutual value
- Recommended outreach approach and talking points for initial contact
- Data access risk assessment: What happens if federations refuse access?

## Research Prompt 2: Pet Market Size & Buyer Behavior in Moldova and Romania

### Research Objective

Quantify the addressable pet market in Moldova and Romania, and validate buyer behavior assumptions underlying PawTrust's go-to-market strategy — specifically whether a sufficient quality-conscious buyer segment exists willing to use a dedicated platform over free alternatives.

### Background Context

PawTrust's Moldova-first launch strategy assumes the Moldovan pet buyer market is large enough to achieve two-sided marketplace critical mass (50 verified breeders + 200 active buyers) within 6 months. This assumption has not been validated with data.

### Core Research Questions

1. How many pet-owning households exist in Moldova and Romania? What is the pet ownership growth trend (2020-2026)?
2. How many new pet purchases (dogs and cats specifically) occur annually in Moldova? In Romania?
3. What percentage of pet purchases currently go through online channels (Facebook, classifieds, Instagram) vs. offline (word of mouth, shows)?
4. What is the price range buyers pay for purebred dogs and cats in Moldova vs. Romania? How price-sensitive are buyers to premium verified listings?
5. Have any pet buyers in Moldova/Romania been scammed or received sick animals through unverified channels? What is the perceived prevalence of fraud?
6. What would motivate a buyer to use a new dedicated platform over a free familiar one (Facebook/999.md)?

### Supporting Questions

- How do Moldovan buyers currently discover breeders? What is the most common first step?
- What breeds are most popular/in-demand in Moldova and Romania currently?
- What is the average household income in Moldova vs. Romania, and what % goes to pet-related spending?
- Are there seasonal patterns to pet purchasing (spring litters, Christmas gifts)?

### Research Methodology

- Search for pet industry reports covering Eastern Europe, Romania, Moldova
- Review national statistics offices for household pet ownership data (INS Romania, BNS Moldova)
- Search for pet food market size reports (proxy for pet ownership data)
- Review press coverage of pet scams/fraud in Moldova and Romania (validates pain point)
- Social media listening: size of active Facebook pet groups in Moldova/Romania (member counts as proxy for market size)

### Output Requirements

- Estimated TAM/SAM for dog and cat purchases annually in Moldova and Romania
- Buyer behavior map: current discovery channels and their usage share
- Price benchmarks: average purebred dog/cat prices in Moldova vs. Romania
- Fraud/scam prevalence assessment with supporting evidence
- Go/no-go recommendation on Moldova-first launch based on market size data
- Critical mass feasibility assessment: is 50 breeders + 200 buyers in 6 months realistic?

## Research Prompt 3: Cat Breeder Organizational Structures in Moldova & Romania

### Research Objective

Understand how cat breeders in Moldova and Romania are organized, what credentials and registrations they use, and what a credible verification framework for cat breeders on PawTrust would look like — given that the cynology/FCI model does not directly apply to cats.

### Background Context

PawTrust's trust model is currently stronger for dog breeders (FCI, cynology competition records) than for cat breeders. Cat breeders operate through different organizational structures. Without a clear cat verification track, PawTrust risks being a dog-centric platform and missing a significant buyer segment.

### Core Research Questions

1. What cat breeder registries and breed clubs are active in Moldova and Romania? (e.g., TICA, WCF, FIFe affiliates)
2. What credentials do serious cat breeders in the region typically hold? What documents do they generate per litter?
3. How do cat shows and competitions work in Moldova/Romania? Are results digitally recorded?
4. What is the market size for purebred cat purchases in Moldova and Romania vs. dogs?
5. What fraud/scam patterns exist specifically in the cat breeder market in EE?
6. What would a credible "Verified Cat Breeder" badge require — what minimum documentation would cat buyers trust?

### Supporting Questions

- Are cat breeders in Moldova/Romania organized into communities (Facebook groups, clubs)?
- What cat breeds are most in demand?
- How do cat breeders currently market and sell kittens?
- Are there certification or health testing standards specific to cats (HCM screening, blood typing)?

### Research Methodology

- Review WCF, FIFe, TICA websites for registered clubs/catteries in Moldova and Romania
- Search Facebook groups for Moldovan/Romanian cat breeders — assess size and activity
- Review 999.md and OLX Romania for cat listings — assess volume vs. dog listings
- Search for cat-specific health testing standards used in EE breeding community

### Output Requirements

- Map of active cat breeder registries and clubs in Moldova and Romania
- Proposed "Verified Cat Breeder" documentation checklist for PawTrust
- Estimated cat vs. dog market share in Moldova/Romania (listings volume proxy)
- Recommended cat breeder onboarding approach that doesn't require cynology infrastructure
- Risk assessment: what happens if cat breeders are excluded or poorly served at launch?

## Research Prompt 4: Pet Sitter Certification Standards in Eastern Europe

### Research Objective

Identify existing pet sitter certification bodies, training programs, and professional standards in Moldova and Romania to define what a credible "Certified Pet Sitter" badge on PawTrust should require — and to assess partnership opportunities with certification providers.

### Background Context

PawTrust plans to differentiate pet sitters through certification badges and verified profiles. Currently, no Eastern European platform offers certified sitter discovery. To make the badge meaningful, it must reference real, recognized standards — not just self-reported claims.

### Core Research Questions

1. What pet sitter certification programs or training courses exist in Moldova and Romania? Who runs them?
2. Are there professional associations for pet sitters or pet care workers in EE? What standards do they set?
3. What certifications do Romanian/Moldovan pet sitters currently seek (first aid, animal behavior, pet care)?
4. What do pet owners in Moldova/Romania currently look for when choosing a pet sitter — what trust signals matter most?
5. Could PawTrust partner with a certification provider to issue badges to course completers automatically?
6. What is the minimum credible certification bar — what would a Moldovan/Romanian pet owner consider a meaningful credential?

### Supporting Questions

- How many active pet sitters operate in Chișinău and Bucharest currently?
- What do pet sitters charge per day/night in Moldova vs. Romania?
- Do pet sitters in the region already use any platform (local or international) to find clients?
- What is the liability/insurance landscape for pet sitters in EE?

### Research Methodology

- Search for pet care/pet sitting courses offered by training providers in Romania and Moldova
- Review veterinary/animal care vocational programs at Romanian/Moldovan universities or colleges
- Search LinkedIn for pet care professionals in Chișinău and Bucharest — what certifications do they list?
- Review Rover's certification requirements as a benchmark standard
- Search Facebook groups for pet sitter communities in Moldova/Romania

### Output Requirements

- List of identified certification programs/bodies in Moldova and Romania with contact info
- Proposed PawTrust "Certified Pet Sitter" badge criteria (minimum viable standard)
- Partnership opportunity assessment with certification providers
- Benchmark: Rover's certification requirements vs. what's available in EE
- Recommended onboarding pathway for sitters who lack formal certification but have experience/references
