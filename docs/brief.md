# Project Brief: PawTrust

## Executive Summary

**PawTrust** is a verification-first web platform connecting trusted dog and cat breeders, pet adopters, and certified pet sitters across Eastern Europe — launching in the Republic of Moldova and expanding to Romania.

**Primary problem solved:** Pet buyers in Eastern Europe have no reliable, purpose-built way to find healthy, ethically bred animals. They are forced to navigate fragmented, unverified channels — Facebook groups, Instagram accounts with random nicknames, and general classifieds like 999.md — with no visibility into breeder credentials, animal health history, or parent quality. This leads to scams, sick animals, and a market dominated by unethical black-market breeders.

**Target market:** Pet buyers, ethical breeders (dogs & cats), and certified pet sitters in Moldova (launch) and Romania (scale-up), with cross-border reach across Eastern Europe.

**Key value proposition:** The only Eastern European platform where trust is built in from the start — breeder cynology competition records, parent health certificates, pedigree data, behavioral/temperament test results, and pet sitter certifications are verified and displayed before a buyer makes a single contact. Unlike Petfinder or general classifieds, PawTrust combines breeder discovery, adoption, and pet sitter services under one verified roof.

**Launch strategy:** Moldova-first as a proving ground (known pain with 999.md/Facebook), with Romanian breeders able to list from day one to solve cross-border supply and price arbitrage. Go/no-go metric for Romania full launch: 50 verified breeders + 200 active buyers within 6 months.

**Verification model:** Tiered trust at launch — Verified listings (badge, featured placement) for breeders with reviewed documents; Unverified listings clearly labeled to ensure supply while incentivizing verification over time.

**MVP scope:** Verified dog and cat breeder profiles + visual listings with parent photos + pay-per-listing monetization. Pet sitter booking, cross-border facilitation, and advanced features follow in v2.

**Future vision:** A regional pet ecosystem including a breed matcher quiz, "Watch this litter" waitlist notifications, an alumni network of happy adopters, and eventually a Pet Passport — a lifetime verified digital profile that travels with every animal on the platform.

## Problem Statement

The current state of pet buying in Eastern Europe is broken.

Pet buyers in Moldova and Romania have three options today: scroll through Facebook groups and Instagram accounts with unverifiable nicknames, browse general classifieds like 999.md where trusted and fraudulent listings sit side by side, or rely on word of mouth from friends. None of these channels provide any meaningful trust signal. Photos are blurry or reused from other sources. Breeders have no credentials displayed. Parent animals are invisible. Prices vary wildly with no quality signal to justify them. Post-purchase, there is no recourse.

**The impact is significant and multi-sided:**

- **Buyers** purchase sick, mislabeled, or fake-pedigree animals — sometimes paying premium prices for animals sourced from puppy mills
- **Ethical breeders** — who invest in cynology competitions, genetic testing, and proper health certification — cannot differentiate themselves from backyard breeders online. They lose sales to cheaper, lower-quality competitors and rely entirely on word of mouth
- **Pet sitters** with real certifications and training have no credible way to signal their quality online beyond Instagram followers
- **The broader ecosystem** suffers: black-market breeders thrive because the information asymmetry favors them

**Why existing solutions fall short:**

- **999.md** is a general classifieds site — pets sit alongside used furniture and job listings, with no category-specific trust infrastructure
- **Facebook/Instagram** are social networks, not marketplaces — no structured search by breed, no verification, no permanence
- **Petfinder** is US-centric, shelter-focused, and culturally irrelevant to Eastern European breeder culture
- **Pawsome and regional equivalents** exist but offer no cynology verification, no parent profiles, no behavioral documentation

**Why now:** Eastern Europe's middle class is growing and pet ownership is rising. Buyers increasingly want quality and are willing to pay for it — but have no trustworthy channel to find it. The cynological infrastructure (FCI-affiliated federations, national breed registries, vet certification systems) already exists in the region. The data exists. The credentials exist. No platform has connected them yet.

## Proposed Solution

**PawTrust is a trust-infrastructure marketplace for pets — not just another classifieds site.**

The core concept is simple: make verified quality the default, not a premium add-on. Every breeder and pet sitter on the platform goes through a document review before receiving a Verified badge. Every listing must include photos of the animal and its parents. Every credential — cynology competition titles, health certificates, temperament test results, pedigree records — is displayed front and center on the listing card, not buried in a profile.

**Key differentiators from existing solutions:**

- **Cynology-native trust signals** — the platform speaks the language of serious Eastern European breeders: FCI titles, national kennel club membership, competition records for parent animals. No other platform in the region integrates this.
- **Visual-first listings** — puppies AND parents are photographed and displayed together. Buyers see exactly what bloodline they are buying into before making a single inquiry.
- **Tiered verification** — Verified listings receive badges and featured placement; Unverified listings are clearly labeled. This ensures supply at launch while creating a natural incentive path toward verification.
- **Separate cat trust track** — recognizing that cat breeders operate differently from the cynology world, cat breeders are verified through vet certifications, cattery registrations, and breed club memberships.
- **Certified pet sitter profiles** — sitters have a care portfolio (photos/videos from past sits), certification badge, real-time availability calendar, and verified reviews — making PawTrust the first Eastern European platform to bring Rover-style credibility to pet care.
- **Cross-border reach** — breeders in Romania can list and reach Moldovan buyers (and vice versa), opening genuine price arbitrage opportunities that word-of-mouth and Instagram cannot provide.

**Why this solution will succeed:**

- The trust infrastructure already exists in the region (FCI, national registries, vet systems) — PawTrust aggregates and surfaces it, it doesn't invent it
- The real competitors are Facebook and 999.md, not specialized platforms — the bar to "better" is genuinely low
- Monetization fits the market culture: pay-per-listing (not subscriptions), pet sitter commission, and partner ads

**High-level product vision:** PawTrust becomes the default starting point for anyone in Eastern Europe who wants to find, adopt, or care for a dog or cat — a platform where every interaction is grounded in verified trust, and where the relationship between breeder and buyer doesn't end at the sale.

## Target Users

### Primary User Segment: Pet Buyers

**Profile:** Adults 25-45 in Moldova and Romania, urban and suburban, middle-income and above, first-time or repeat pet owners. Increasingly connected online but frustrated by the quality and safety of current channels.

**Current behaviors:** Searching Facebook groups by breed name, scrolling 999.md pet categories, asking friends for breeder recommendations, occasionally importing from abroad via word of mouth.

**Specific needs & pain points:**

- Cannot verify breeder legitimacy before paying deposit
- No visibility into parent animals' health or appearance
- No structured search by breed, location, or price range
- Fear of buying a sick animal or being scammed after payment
- No way to distinguish ethical breeders from backyard/puppy mill operators

**Goals:** Find a healthy, well-bred dog or cat from a trustworthy source, with confidence before visiting or paying.

---

### Secondary User Segment: Breeders (Dogs & Cats)

**Profile:** Serious hobbyist to semi-professional breeders in Moldova, Romania, and broader Eastern Europe. Dog breeders are often FCI-affiliated with competition history; cat breeders have breed club membership or vet-certified catteries. Sell 1-5 litters per year.

**Current behaviors:** Word-of-mouth sales, personal Facebook/Instagram profiles, occasional listings on 999.md. Breeders with competition credentials have no platform to showcase them.

**Specific needs & pain points:**

- No way to differentiate their quality and investment online
- Limited reach — local/national only, missing cross-border buyers willing to pay more
- Compete directly on price against unethical breeders with no way to justify premium
- Repeat WhatsApp/Viber inquiries about the same basic questions (parents, health, price)

**Goals:** Attract serious, educated buyers willing to pay fair prices; build a credible online presence that reflects years of investment in quality breeding.

---

### Secondary User Segment: Certified Pet Sitters

**Profile:** Individual pet sitters in Moldova and Romania with some form of certification (first aid, pet care courses) or professional experience. Currently operate via Instagram, word of mouth, or local Facebook groups. 1-5 pets at a time.

**Current behaviors:** Building clientele manually through personal networks; no discovery mechanism beyond social media presence.

**Specific needs & pain points:**

- Invisible to pet owners who don't already know them
- No platform to display certifications, portfolio, or availability professionally
- Compete with uncertified, cheaper sitters with no quality signal to justify pricing
- Managing bookings manually via Viber/WhatsApp

**Goals:** Build a sustainable business with steady bookings from quality-conscious pet owners; be found by the right clients online.

## Goals & Success Metrics

### Business Objectives

- Achieve 50 verified breeders + 200 active buyers in Moldova within 6 months of launch (go/no-go threshold for Romania expansion)
- Generate first revenue within 30 days of launch via pay-per-listing fees
- Onboard 20 certified pet sitters in Moldova within 3 months of launch
- Reach break-even on operational costs within 12 months of Moldova launch
- Launch Romanian market within 9-12 months of Moldova launch

### User Success Metrics

- Buyers can find a verified breeder for their target breed within 5 minutes of landing on the platform
- 80%+ of buyers who contact a breeder through PawTrust report feeling confident in the breeder's legitimacy
- Breeders report receiving at least 1 qualified inquiry per listing within 2 weeks of posting
- Pet sitters receive at least 1 booking within their first month on the platform
- 0 confirmed fraud cases involving Verified-badged listings in the first year

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

- **Verified breeder count:** Number of breeders with Verified badge — target 50 in Moldova at 6 months
- **Active listings:** Number of live pet listings at any time — target 100+ at 3 months
- **Buyer-to-inquiry rate:** % of buyers who view a listing and send an inquiry — target 15%+
- **Listing fee revenue:** Monthly recurring revenue from pay-per-listing — target €500/month at 3 months
- **Verification conversion rate:** % of unverified breeders who achieve Verified status within 90 days — target 60%
- **Pet sitter booking rate:** % of active sitters with at least 1 completed booking per month — target 50%
- **Return visitor rate:** % of users returning within 30 days — target 40%+

## MVP Scope

### Core Features (Must Have)

- **User registration & profiles:** Buyers, Breeders, and Pet Sitters can create accounts with role-specific profile pages
- **Breeder listing creation:** Breeders can post pet listings with multiple photos (puppies + both parents required), breed, age, price, location, and document uploads (health certificates, cynology records, temperament tests)
- **Tiered verification system:** Admin workflow for reviewing uploaded documents; Verified badge granted on approval; Unverified listings clearly labeled with "Pending Verification" state
- **Search & filter:** Buyers can search by species (dog/cat), breed, location, and verification status
- **Inquiry system:** Buyer expresses interest → breeder's Viber/WhatsApp contact revealed; basic in-platform message to initiate contact
- **Pet sitter profiles:** Sitters can create a profile with care portfolio photos, certification uploads, and contact details (booking calendar deferred to v2)
- **Pay-per-listing fee:** Breeders pay a small fee (2-5€) to publish each listing; payment via local gateway
- **Admin verification dashboard:** Internal tool for reviewing and approving/rejecting breeder document submissions
- **Romanian & Russian language support:** Platform available in Romanian and Russian (critical for Moldova market)

### Out of Scope for MVP

- Pet sitter booking calendar and commission payment flow
- Live video call integration
- Breed matcher quiz
- "Watch this litter" waitlist/notification system
- Pet Passport (lifetime digital profile)
- Cross-border payment facilitation
- API integrations with cynology federations or national registries
- Partner/vet ad system
- Mobile app (web only)
- Community forum or alumni network

### MVP Success Criteria

The MVP is considered successful when: at least 50 verified breeders are active in Moldova, at least 200 buyers have registered and made at least one inquiry, pay-per-listing revenue is generating consistent monthly income, and zero fraud incidents have been reported on Verified listings — all within 6 months of launch. This unlocks the decision to expand to Romania.

## Post-MVP Vision

### Phase 2 Features

- **Pet sitter booking system** — Full calendar availability, in-platform booking, and commission-based payment processing (platform takes % per booking)
- **Breed matcher quiz** — Lifestyle questionnaire matching buyers to suitable breeds and verified breeders with active listings
- **"Watch this litter" waitlist** — Buyers register interest in a breed, get notified when a verified breeder posts a matching litter
- **Partner/vet ad placements** — Vet clinics, pet food brands, and pet accessory shops pay for targeted placement to a highly relevant audience
- **Featured Kennel spotlight** — Paid premium placement; one breeder per breed per region featured on homepage monthly
- **Romania full launch** — Dedicated Romanian market with local SEO, Romanian breeder onboarding, and local payment methods

### Long-term Vision (1-2 years)

PawTrust becomes the trusted pet ecosystem for Eastern Europe — the platform where the entire pet ownership journey lives, from finding and verifying a breeder, to booking a certified pet sitter, to connecting with a vet partner. At scale, PawTrust expands to Poland, Czech Republic, Slovakia, Hungary, Bulgaria, and Ukraine — becoming the authoritative, trust-first destination for pet discovery in the region.

Revenue diversifies from pay-per-listing into a healthy mix of sitter commissions, optional premium breeder features, and B2B partnerships. The platform pursues API partnerships with national cynology federations and breed registries to automate verification and become the authoritative source of pet provenance data in the region.

### Expansion Opportunities

- **Geographic expansion** — Poland, Czech Republic, Slovakia, Hungary, Bulgaria, Ukraine (each with FCI-affiliated clubs and strong cynological culture)
- **Pet Passport** — A lifetime verified digital profile for every animal listed, transferable to new owners, integrating vet records and ownership history
- **Alumni network** — "Happy adopters" public feed of pets bought through PawTrust, creating organic social proof and discovery content
- **Certification course marketplace** — Partner with or host pet sitter certification courses; completion automatically grants profile badge
- **B2G potential** — Animal welfare authorities in EU-adjacent countries may fund or partner on verified breeder registries as a public good

## Technical Considerations

### Platform Requirements

- **Target Platforms:** Web only (desktop + mobile-responsive browser)
- **Browser/OS Support:** Modern browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge)
- **Performance Requirements:** Page load <3 seconds; photo galleries optimized for mobile data speeds

### Technology Preferences

- **Frontend:** React (component-based architecture suits listing cards, profile pages, search/filter UI)
- **Backend:** PHP (Laravel recommended for structured API, auth, and admin workflows)
- **Database:** PostgreSQL or MySQL — relational structure suits users/listings/documents/reviews
- **File Storage:** AWS S3 for photos and uploaded certification documents
- **Hosting/Infrastructure:** AWS (EC2 or Elastic Beanstalk for application, S3 for assets, RDS for database, CloudFront CDN for photo delivery speed in Eastern Europe)
- **Payment:** Stripe (international) + local Moldovan/Romanian gateway research needed (MAIB, Mobiasbancă, or similar)

### Architecture Considerations

- **Repository Structure:** Separate frontend (React) and backend (PHP/Laravel) repositories
- **Service Architecture:** Monolith at MVP stage — microservices add unnecessary complexity before product-market fit
- **Integration Requirements:**
  - Local payment gateway (Moldova + Romania)
  - Email notifications (listing approved/rejected, inquiry received)
  - Future: cynology federation APIs (v2+)
- **Security/Compliance:**
  - GDPR compliance required for Romanian users (EU)
  - Document uploads stored securely in AWS S3 with bucket policies; access restricted to admins and listing owner only
  - Breeder contact details not publicly exposed — revealed only after buyer sends inquiry (prevents scraping/spam)
  - Photo watermarking recommended to prevent listing fraud

## Risks & Open Questions

### Key Risks

- **Two-sided cold start:** No breeders = no buyers, no buyers = no breeders. The marketplace never reaches viable density without deliberate supply-side seeding before launch.
- **Fake/fraudulent listings:** One publicized scam on the platform destroys the core trust promise entirely.
- **Breeder resistance to digital onboarding:** Especially cat breeders and older cynologists who have never used a digital platform — friction in the verification process could limit supply quality at launch.
- **Federation data not digitized:** Cynology federation records may not be accessible via API, requiring indefinite manual verification — a process that doesn't scale without hiring.
- **Moldova market too small:** The Moldovan pet buyer market may not reach critical mass fast enough to sustain two-sided growth momentum.
- **Cross-border regulatory barriers:** Live animal transport between Moldova (non-EU) and Romania (EU) requires veterinary export certificates and customs processes — legal complexity not yet researched.
- **Platform misused by black market breeders:** Unverified tier could be gamed by bad actors to gain superficial legitimacy through the platform's brand.

### Open Questions

- Is the Moldovan pet buyer market large enough to achieve two-sided critical mass within 6 months?
- What are the exact live animal transport regulations between Moldova and Romania?
- What certification bodies or standards exist for pet sitters in Moldova and Romania?
- Should rescues/shelters be supported on the platform in addition to breeders, or stay breeder-only for MVP?
- What is the minimum credible trust bar for cat breeders with no cynology affiliation?
- How do we handle post-purchase disputes (e.g., buyer claims puppy was sick)?
- What local payment gateways are available and trusted in Moldova?
- Should the platform operate under a Moldovan or Romanian legal entity first?

### Areas Needing Further Research

- Live animal transport laws and veterinary export requirements Moldova ↔ Romania ↔ EU
- Competitive landscape: any emerging regional players in Romania, Poland, or Czech Republic targeting this space
- Local payment gateway options for Moldova (MAIB, Mobiasbancă, etc.) and their API/integration capabilities
- Cat breeder organizational structures in Moldova and Romania (breed clubs, cattery registrations)
- GDPR applicability and compliance requirements for a Moldova-based company processing Romanian (EU) user data
- Cynology federation digitization status in target launch countries (Moldova, Romania)
